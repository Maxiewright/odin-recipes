Description

Trinidad Doubles are the most popular street food found all over both Trinidad and Tobago. It is a popular breakfast food as well as eaten as a street snack anytime of the day or a late-night favorite. They are often served on parchment paper or waxed paper with a squeeze of lemon juice or lime juice.

Ingredients

Ingredients for the Bara's

  • 2 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 2 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric
  • 1 cup lukewarm water +/-
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil for rubbing on the dough
  • 2 cups vegetable oil for frying the bara's.

Ingredients for the Channa (Chickpea filling)

  • 1/2 lb. chickpeas, dried
  • 1 tsp baking soda divided
  • 1 Tbsp culantro minced
  • 3 cloves garlic minced
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric use more or less according to taste
  • 1/2 tsp cumin, ground roasted
  • 1 tsp salt +/- to taste

Steps

Making the Bara's

  1. In a medium bowl, add the flour, salt, yeast, sugar and turmeric, mix together.
  2. Add lukewarm water slowly and mix together to form a soft, slightly sticky dough. Do not over mix.
  3. Rub the dough with oil and set aside to rest for 6 hours.
  4. Divide the dough into 16 balls.
  5. Cover the balls and allow to raise for another hour or until doubled in size.
  6. Rub some oil on a plate. Flatten the dough out to a round to about the size of the palm of your hand.
  7. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat.
  8. When the oil is hot, place a piece of the dough in hot oil and fry until golden brown, then flip over to fry the other side. Remove with a slotted spoon to drain off as much oil as possible and place on a paper towel.
  9. Repeat until all the dough is cooked stacking the Bara's one on top of the other.
  10. Serve hot with the Channa recipe.

Steps for the Channa filling

  1. Soak the dried chickpeas while the dough is rising about 6 hours or overnight in water with 1/2 tsp of baking soda. Drain and rinse several times.
  2. In a large pot add about 6 cups of water and place over high heat. Add the chickpeas, I teaspoon of salt, 1/2 teaspoon baking soda and stir to combine. Bring to a boil and skim off any impurities that rise to the surface. Reduce heat to medium-low.
  3. Cook until tender about 1 hour, add minced culantro, minced garlic and turmeric, mix well. If you are adding in any hot peppers you can add it here.
  4. Cook for another 15 minutes or so stirring with a wooden spoon until you get a nice thick consistency.
  5. Add in the ground roasted cumin and taste for salt.
  6. Serve on the hot Bara's and serve with your chutney of choice enjoy your doubles!
